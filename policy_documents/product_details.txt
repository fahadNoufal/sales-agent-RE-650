PRODUCT SPECIFICATION: ROYAL ENFIELD BULLET 650 (2026)

1. PRODUCT OVERVIEW:
The Bullet 650 is the most powerful iteration of the iconic "Bullet" nameplate in its 90-year history. It combines the legendary silhouette of the Bullet 350 with the award-winning 650cc parallel-twin platform.

2. ENGINE & PERFORMANCE:
- Engine Type: 648cc, Parallel-twin, 4-stroke, SOHC, Air-Oil Cooled.
- Max Power: 47.04 PS @ 7250 rpm.
- Max Torque: 52.3 Nm @ 5650 rpm.
- Gearbox: 6-speed constant mesh with Slip-and-Assist clutch.
- Exhaust: Signature twin peashooter exhausts tuned for a deep, rhythmic "thump."
- Top Speed: Approx. 150-160 km/h.

3. DESIGN & CHARACTERISTICS:
- Frame: Steel Tubular Spine Frame (derived from the Shotgun/Super Meteor 650).
- Signature Elements: Teardrop fuel tank with hand-painted pinstripes, winged badge, and the iconic "Tiger Eye" pilot lamps.
- Seat: Deeply padded, stepped bench seat designed for long-distance touring and pillion comfort.
- Build: Heavy-duty metal body panels for a solid, "built like a gun" feel.
- Weight: Kerb weight of 243 kg, providing massive highway stability.

4. CHASSIS, BRAKES & WHEELS:
- Front Suspension: 43mm Telescopic forks (120mm travel).
- Rear Suspension: Twin shock absorbers with adjustable preload (90mm travel).
- Brakes: 320mm front disc / 300mm rear disc.
- ABS: Dual-channel ABS as standard.
- Wheels: Classic wire-spoke wheels.
- Tyres: 19-inch Front (100/90) and 18-inch Rear (140/70) for a commanding stance.

5. MODERN FEATURES:
- Lighting: LED Headlamp (casquette mounted) and LED Tail lamp.
- Instrument Cluster: Digianalogue console with LCD multi-info display.
- Navigation: Optional Tripper Navigation pod.
- Charging: Built-in USB-C charging port for mobile devices.

6. PRICING & AVAILABILITY (INDIA 2026):
- Expected Price: ₹2.80 Lakh - ₹3.10 Lakh (Ex-showroom).
- Launch Window: February/March 2026.
- Color Options: Cannon Black, Battleship Blue, and premium Chrome editions.

7. RIDER BENEFIT SUMMARY:
The Bullet 650 is for the rider who refuses to compromise on heritage but demands modern highway capability. It offers the "soul" of a classic Bullet with the vibration-free, high-speed refinement of a modern twin-cylinder machine.
